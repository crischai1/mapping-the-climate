This is a heatmap displaying the average temperature across the United States. Generally, points closer to the equator tend to be hotter and points further tend to be colder. This is due to the fact that the sun strikes the equator at the sharpest possible angle. Hence, the further a plot is from the equator, the less sharp this striking angle becomes and the less dense the heat is, resulting in a less hot temperature.
